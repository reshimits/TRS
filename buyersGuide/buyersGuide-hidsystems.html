How much light output do you need?

35 Watt Ballast

Overall: More efficient, longer lasting. Industry Standard in all vehicles that come factory-equipped with HID headlights. Best for use on vehicles with Canbus systems or weaker than average electrical systems (old vehicles, motorcycles, etc).
Lumens: 5800-6200 Lifespan: ~2,000 hrs Power Consumption: average Color Shift: none

55 Watt Ballast

Overall: Brighter, more power hungry, lesser lifespan. Produces more output compared to all factory-installed HID headlight ballasts. Not recommended without the use of a relayed wire harness or in smaller projector and/or housings (fog lights).
Lumens: 9100-9700 Lifespan: ~1,500 hrs Power Consumption: above average Color Shift: yes

Light Output: 35W

What Color is right for you?

3000K (Golden Yellow)

~2800 lumens (35w) Recommended for fog lights only, the yellow light penetrates through particles in the air more effectively than white light does. Great for driving in rain, snow, and fog.

4300K (Warm White)

~3400 lumens (35w) Vehicles with factory installed xenon headlights use 4300K. Best for all-season driving (mix of clear and poor weather).

5000K (Pure White)

~3300 lumens (35w) Our most popular, though slightly less lumens, most people find the increase in contrast at from the whiter light improves overall vision.

6000K (Blue Tinge)

~3100 lumens (35w) For those looking seeking a more stand-out look compared to stock, without over-compromising the amount of useful light output.

Light Output: 35W
